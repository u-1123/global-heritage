Global Digital Heritage (GDH) is a not-for-profit, private research and education organization dedicated to documenting, monitoring, and preserving our global cultural and natural heritage.

We use digital visualization, 3D virtualization, geospatial informatics, and open access solutions to provide digital data and 3D models to governments, regional institutions, museums, local scholars, and the public. A key element of our mission is the Openness of science—we make all data freely available to the world in support of cultural heritage, heritage management, education, public access, scientific research, and to enhance the digital humanities.


The Colosseum

Located in Rome, Italy, the Colosseum is a medieval amphitheater primarily used for gladiatorial contests and theater. It was built in 75 BC and is the oldest surviving amphitheater.

The Colosseum-Rome
The Colosseum has been the world’s largest amphitheater since it was built more than 2,000 years ago. When it was first opened in AD 80, this huge imposing structure measured 150 meters wide and 190 meters long, with 80 arched entrances and capacity to seat up to 74,000 spectators.

Machu Picchu’s-Peru

Situated at an altitude of about 2,500 meters above sea level, this ancient Inca city in Peru boasts a breathtaking location in the midst of a tropical forest. Built during the 15th century, Machu Picchu’s outstanding structures including La Ciudadela throw light on the Inca Empire’s complex yet refined architecture.

Greatest Ancient

The Old Kingdom (c. 2686–2181 BCE): Known as the "Age of the Pyramids," this period saw the construction of the Great Pyramid of Giza and the Sphinx under the pharaoh Khufu.

Great Ancient-EGypt

Ancient Egypt is one of the world's most fascinating and influential civilizations, with a history spanning over 3,000 years. From the early dynasties to the final conquest by Alexander the Great, The unification of Upper and Lower Egypt is attributed to King Narmer (also known as Menes), who is considered the first pharaoh. This marks the beginning of Egypt's dynastic period.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is one of the most iconic landmarks in the world, located in Agra, India. It was built by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan in memory of his beloved wife Mumtaz Mahal.

TAJ MAHAL-INDIA
Construction of the Taj Mahal began in 1632, and it was completed in 1653, taking around 21 years. The monument is renowned for its stunning white marble architecture, intricate carvings, and beautiful gardens. It is a symbol of love and a masterpiece of Mughal architecture, blending Persian, Ottoman Turkish, and Indian architectural styles. The Taj Mahal is listed as a UNESCO World Heritage site and is visited by millions of tourists every year. Its striking beauty and historical significance make it one of the Seven Wonders of the World. The Dome and Minarets: The central dome of the Taj Mahal is 35 meters high and is surrounded by four minarets (tall towers) which enhance its symmetry. The dome’s design represents the perfection and infinity of the divine. The minarets were constructed with a slight tilt to ensure they would never fall on the main structure in case of an earthquake.
